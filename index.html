Hic Scientia
Futūrum Creat

"Where knowledge creates the future"

KIU opened to its first cohort in 2020 as Georgia's newest public university, built on a 150-hectare forested campus outside Kutaisi. Its operating model and curricula are developed in partnership with the Technical University of Munich (TUM), with TUM's former president serving as KIU's honorary president — backed by a multi-decade investment from the Cartu Foundation.

A Young University, Moving Fast

Milestones

  1. 2020

    First cohort enrolls

    KIU opens its doors on the new Kutaisi campus, becoming a Legal Entity of Public Law — a Georgian state university.

  2. 2020–

    TUM partnership begins

    The operating model and academic programs are co-developed with the Technical University of Munich and TUM International GmbH. Prof. Dr. Wolfgang A. Herrmann, TUM's former president, becomes KIU's honorary president.

  3. 2020–

    Cartu Foundation investment

    The Cartu Foundation commits long-term funding toward building KIU into a regional hub for education, science and technology.

  4. 2025

    Ten degree programs

    The academic offer grows to ten English- and Georgian-taught programs across five schools, from Computer Science to Medicine.

  5. Today

    Research infrastructure online

    Facilities including a hadron-therapy and cyclotron centre support physics, biophysics and radiochemistry research alongside a student-run innovation ecosystem.

    International Partnership

    Built with the Technical University of Munich

    KIU's curricula, governance model and quality standards are developed jointly with TUM and TUM International GmbH — one of Europe's leading technical universities. Many of KIU's international faculty come from Germany as part of this partnership, and TUM's former president, Prof. Dr. Wolfgang A. Herrmann, holds the role of KIU's honorary president.

    The campus itself sits about 25 km from Kutaisi International Airport and roughly a three-hour drive from Tbilisi — close enough to the capital, remote enough to be its own quiet research community by the river.
